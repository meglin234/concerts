Dear Data

A Week of Communication

Composed, Arranged, and Produced by Megan Church

Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec developed a friendship through their project Dear Data. Each week, for a year, they collected and measured a particular type of data about their lives and used this data to create a visualization on a postcard. Their goal was to show that instead of using data to become more efficient, we can use data to become more humane and connect with ourselves and others at a deeper level.

Having an interest in how people interact with data and their surroundings, I decided to collect data about how often I communicated for a week and created my very own hand-drawn visualization. Since my data is about communication, a collection of interactions with others that fit together, I decided to visualize my data as a collection of sounds that fit together to create music.

The Data

For one week, I recorded every time I communicated with someone. Therefore, the data only represents times I spoke or texted; it does not include times when other people spoke to or texted me. For that week, I recorded the person I communicated with and if I spoke to or texted them. I also noted what social media platform or messaging app I used, the day of the week, and time of day I communicated with them.

Since there are only seven different notes on the staff in any given key, I organized my data into seven groups: roommate 1, roommate 2, brother, parents, classmates, research group, and others. The other category includes group chats, my study abroad program, and strangers.

Once who I communicated with was established, I moved on to how. Talk was easy, I either spoke to them in person or over the phone, and since the purpose for communication was the same either way I decided not to distinguish between the two. Text on the other hand, is slightly more complicated. The majority of the time I was simply texting over iMessage, however, communication changes when it's over social media. With Instagram, for example, I'll direct message (dm) a meme or video to a friend with no accompanying text, and although the content of the dm isn't mine, we are still communicating. This difference in communication styles led me to add different articulation to notes depending on what messaging/social media app I used.

The Visualization

Different people, or groups of people, I communicated with are represented through different notes. The way I communicated with them, either talk or text, is shown by the color of the note. Lastly, if I communicated with someone through text, the messaging/social media app is denoted using articulation.

- Each score is a different day

- Each measure is an hour in the day

- Each note is a 20-minute interval in the hour

The time signature is in 3/4 because it allowed me to divide the hour into three 20-minute intervals, while keeping the beats quarter notes. Plus, 3/4 is my favorite time signature so I had to make it the foundation of my conversations.

If I communicated with someone within a 20-minute interval, or one beat, their note, color, and articulation was recorded as a quarter note. If I communicated with two people during the 20-minute interval I used 8th notes (two notes in one beat) to distinguish them from each other. There were a few cases where I communicated with more than two people in a 20-minute interval and they are recorded as 16th notes (four notes in one beat). If I spoke with someone for more that the 20-minute interval I recorded it as a half note or a dotted half note (two beats in one note).

Notes that are tied together or marked to be slurred show a conversation that bridged the hour mark or that was interrupted by my communicating with someone else.

Conclusions

After collecting the data and creating visualizations, I can conclude that I communicate with roommate 2 the most. I am not surprised by this finding since they are also my best friend, but it is quite interesting to see when we communicate and how. You may also notice that there are only a few pink notes in the mornings, showing that I do not talk that much until the evening. This would be because I was either in class all day or my roommates were in class all day.

A few other things that might catch your attention:

- My primary method of text communication is through iMessage

- I always GroupMe my classmates

- I tend to use Instagram at night and Twitter during the day

- On Saturday nights my brother and I watch Moon Knight

Notes

On Wednesday, I would usually have a research meeting from 12pm to 1pm. However, this particular Wednesday, I attended a training at the School of Education on qualitative data methods with my research group from 10am to 3pm, so I spoke more than usual.

On Friday and Saturday, both of my roommates were out of town, leaving me all alone with the cat. In hindsight, I could have included Sunny as a "person" I communicate with, but I'm not sure if I was actually communicating with him or if I was just speaking to myself. Plus, considering the number of times I speak to him, I would have needed a whole other postcard just for him.
